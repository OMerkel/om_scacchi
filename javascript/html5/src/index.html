Rules

om_scacchi plays standard FIDE chess. White moves first. Click a piece to select it, then click a destination square to move.

Piece Movement
  • King – one square in any direction; castling allowed when path is clear and not in check.
  • Queen – any number of squares diagonally, horizontally, or vertically.
  • Rook – any number of squares horizontally or vertically.
  • Bishop – any number of squares diagonally.
  • Knight – L-shape (2+1 squares); the only piece that can jump over others.
  • Pawn – one square forward (two from starting rank); captures diagonally one square forward.
Special Moves
  • Castling – King moves two squares toward the rook; rook jumps to the other side. Conditions: neither piece has moved, no pieces between them, king not in check and does not pass through check.
  • En passant – A pawn that just advanced two squares can be captured by an opposing pawn as if it had only moved one square (available only immediately after the double advance).
  • Promotion – A pawn reaching the 8th rank must promote to queen, rook, bishop, or knight.
Game End and Draws
  • Checkmate – King is in check and has no legal move; the attacking side wins.
  • Stalemate – Side to move has no legal move but is not in check; draw.
  • 50-move rule – 50 moves without a pawn move or capture; draw.
  • Threefold repetition – Same position occurs three times; draw.
  • Insufficient material – Neither side can force checkmate; draw.
